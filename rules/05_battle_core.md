# PART III — BATTLE CORE

## Chapter 5 — Turn Structure & Segments (AP)
Combat is measured in **6-segment rounds**. Time and Stamina (SP) are fungible; every 1-segment of action costs 1 SP.
- **Major Action (4 Segments / 4 SP):** Heavy swings, complex spells, First Aid.
- **Minor Action (2 Segments / 2 SP):** Defensive footwork, feints, shield-locking, standing up.
- **Quick Action (1 Segment / 1 SP):** Drawing a blade, shifting facing, moving 1 Hex.
- **Reactions (Variable Segments / 2 SP Base):** Parries, dodges, or diving. Reactions consume segments from your banked turn.

## Chapter 6 — The 3-Stage Cascading Roll
To maintain simulation depth, every combat encounter resolves through three distinct 3d6 rolls. Interactive sheets automate the math; players provide the dice.

### 1. Skill Roll (Accuracy vs Defense)
`3d6 + Skill + DEX vs Target Defense`
- **Fail:** Attack is parried or misses entirely.
- **Partial/Success:** Transition to Stage 2. Margins of success provide +1 to +3 Quality bonuses.

### 2. Quality Roll (Impact Severity)
`3d6 + Relative Force + Quality Bonus`
- **Impact Severity (IS):** The raw kinetic energy transferred.
- **IS Tiers:**
  - 1–10: Surface Glancing
  - 11–15: Solid Impact
  - 16–20: Crushing Blow
  - 21+: Overwhelming Breach

### 3. Consequence Roll (The Kinetic Transfer)
`3d6 + IS vs Armor HP Resist`
- **HP Damage (Lethal):** Any IS exceeding the Armor's `HP Resist`.
- **SP Damage (Shock):** Any IS successfully absorbed by the armor is converted into **Shock (SP)**.
- **Injury/Status:** High rolls (Success/Perfect) on this stage trigger specific injuries (Bleeding, Stunned, Knocked Down).

## Chapter 7 — Facing and Positional Exposure
Every unit occupies a single hex and has a strict **Facing Direction**.
- **Front (3 Hexes):** Full defense; all reactions and shields apply.
- **Flank (2 Side Hexes):** Partial exposure; attacker gains +2 to Stage 1 Skill Roll.
- **Rear (1 Hex):** Major exposure; No shield use, No reactions, attacker gains +4 to Stage 1.

## Chapter 8 — Momentum & Acceleration
- **Acceleration Tax:** Moving from a standstill requires a 1nd-segment Quick Action tax.
- **Momentum:** Moving in a straight line for 4+ hexes adds +1 to +3 IS (Stage 2).
