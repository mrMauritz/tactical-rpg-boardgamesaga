# PART IV — ADVENTURE & INTERACTION

## Chapter 12 — The Prep Phase (The Camping Tool)
Before combat begins, the party enters a **Prep Phase** to establish grounded **Physical Pre-Conditions** on the map. This is a tactical toolbox:
- **Scouting:** Reveals the enemy `Facing` and `RF Tiers` (Saga-accurate intel).
- **Sabotage:** Assigns the `Unstable` or `Damaged` Physical Tag to a hex-structure (e.g., bridge or pillar).
- **Environment Manipulation:** Using items or skills to change a hex (e.g., spilling oil to create a `Slippery` hex *before* the first round).

## Chapter 13 — Material-Based Interactivity & Event Hexes
Map interaction is governed by **Material Tags**, allowing for grounded physics:
- **[Wood]:** Flammable, Low Structural BP.
- **[Stone]:** High Stability, Brittle against heavy momentum.
- **[Iron]:** Extreme Resist, conducts Heat/Arcane.
- **Interactivity:** A Heavy Action (4 segments) can target a Material's structural integrity using the standard 3-stage resolution.

## Chapter 15 — World-Seed Resolution (Dual-Layer Propagation)
Every combat outcome is **seeded** back into the Campaign Map:
- **Reality Layer:** Physical destruction (e.g., breaking the bridge) immediately updates the Hex Tag (e.g., `Bridge` -> `Broken`). 
- **Wyrd Layer:** Mythic deeds or tragedies are "seeded" and may inspire players in the current or future Saga (e.g., an "Inspired Story" that leads to a new character path).
- **Destruction is Propagation:** The map is not a static backdrop; it is the physical record of the Saga's violence.

## Chapter 14 — Dynamic Terrain States
The environment is not just a backdrop; it is a mutable tactical factor.

- **State Transitions:** Hexes can transition through interaction (Damage, Magic, or Time).
    - *Example:* Thicket (Cover) + Fire -> Ashen (No Cover/Low Movement Cost).
    - *Example:* Stone Floor + Oil -> Slippery (Lose Balance Points).
- **Persistence:** These changes remain for the duration of the encounter, permanently altering the tactical flow.
